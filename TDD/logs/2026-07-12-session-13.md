## What was accomplished

### Collage capture + extraction (130-sticker library)

- **Capture workflow settled by experiment.** Owner's first idea (white pieces laid on grid paper, one photo) was run to its honest limit: extraction worked (8/8 drawings incl. two pencil), but curled-paper **crease shadows are locally indistinguishable from bold strokes** — 4 ghost pieces, shadow bands, one shadow-bridge merge. `--shadow-filter` (local-darkness test) recovered ~70%, not enough. **Dark-background re-shoot became the standard** and was flawless: shadows fall on dark cloth, invisible to bright-paper piece detection; every batch after came out clean.
- **`clean_scan.py` grew:** `--keep-color` (collage mode: per-channel flat-field distance keeps every pen color incl. dark blue; used by elements mode), `--shadow-filter`, **binder-hole disk filter** (solid near-circular blobs 40–140 px dropped automatically — line art never makes solid disks; caught 20+ punch holes), auto **contact sheet** written next to the output dir after every run.
- **Grid-paper batches:** the blue-grid suppression rule missed real photos — **JPEG chroma subsampling + white balance desaturate thin grid lines to GREY**, so the "bluish AND light" test fails. Fix: darkness threshold (`--paper-tol 0.18 --noise-floor 0.3`) — pen ink is 3–4× darker than grid residue. Now the default in `process_incoming.py` (grid paper is the stack standard).
- **`process_incoming.py`:** drop photos in `art_drops/collage/incoming/`, run, processed photos move to `done/` — re-runs only touch new photos (owner rejected a hash-manifest design as over-engineered; the folder-move is the whole mechanism). Regenerates the full-library contact sheet each run.
- **Result: 16 photos → 132 stickers → 130 after owner culled 2.** One photo was a multi-drawing scene that split into several stickers — owner kept the split.

### Custom font (owner-approved)

- **`extract_glyphs.py`:** page → ink → **enclosed CELLS** (box interiors) → glyph = ink inside cell; naturally immune to binder holes/table/stray marks. `--close-gaps` ink dilation makes hand-drawn boxes with open corners count as enclosed (fixed rows losing cells → mapping shifts). Per-glyph despeckle: `MULTIPART_CHARS` (i j ! ? : ; " % = ' . ,) keep small parts; all other chars keep only components ≥25% of the main body (kills flecks that inflated bboxes and advance widths — the "ho.st / cu.stom" dots).
- **Charset mapping:** owner's own box order, NOT the guide's; upper-page row 6 is `( ) _ ^ _` (initially miscatalogued as `& ^ _ ( )` — every paren rendered as a caret until fixed; diagnosed off the PIL specimen). **81 glyphs:** A–Z a–z 0–9 `! ? " # % @ < > * ^ ~ : ; ( ) [ ] { }`.
- **`build_font.py` + `ff_assemble.py`** (potrace + fontforge, brew-installed with owner consent): PNG→PBM→potrace SVG (small glyphs upscaled ≥400px before tracing)→FontForge with bbox-normalized import. **Metrics: typographic CLASS normalization** — owner explicitly rejected as-drawn sizing ("ransom note"): caps 660, digits 640, x-letters 460, ascenders 680, descenders 640/−200, per-char table for symbols. Letterforms preserved, size wobble removed.
- **Wired as theme `default_font`.** Undrawn characters (`. , ' - & + =` etc.) render via Godot's system-font fallback (same mechanism as the emoji) until the owner draws them in spare boxes.

## Issues / gotchas (for future art sessions)

- **Godot canvas_item shaders: fragment `COLOR` arrives ALREADY multiplied by `texture(TEXTURE, UV)`** — overwrite it, never multiply, or you render the intersection of two samples (wallpaper showed only overlapping strokes).
- **Control-position animation snaps to whole pixels** → jitter at slow speeds; scroll UVs in a shader instead.
- **JPEG phone photos desaturate thin blue grid lines to grey** — color-based grid suppression fails; separate by darkness instead.
- **Curled paper on a light background = crease shadows that look like ink.** Dark background, not software, is the fix.
- Normalizing glyphs by bbox makes them **hyper-sensitive to any stray ink in the box** — despeckle before measuring.
